1. Overview

The Restaurant Ordering System is committed to responsible data handling practices. This policy outlines how we collect, process, store, and protect data for restaurant owners (hotels), customers, and staff members.

üîí Our Commitment: We follow industry best practices for data protection and comply with global data privacy regulations.

2. Data Categories We Handle

2.1 Restaurant Owner Data

  • Business name, address, phone number
  • Owner/Manager contact information
  • Bank account details (for payments)
  • Menu and pricing information
  • Table configurations
  • Order history and analytics
  • Subscription and payment records

2.2 Customer Data

  • Customer names and phone numbers
  • Order history and preferences
  • Table number and visit timestamp
  • Payment information (for online orders)
  • Feedback and ratings

2.3 Staff Data

  • Staff login credentials
  • Role and permission information
  • Activity logs (who accessed what)

3. Data Minimization Principle

We collect only the minimum data necessary to provide our service. We do not collect unnecessary personal information and regularly review our data collection practices.

What We DON'T Collect:

  • Biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition)
  • Health or dietary preference data (unless voluntarily provided)
  • Credit card information directly (processed through Razorpay)
  • Browsing history or device IDs
  • Location data beyond restaurant location

4. Data Storage & Encryption

5. Access Control & Authorization

5.1 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

We implement strict role-based access control to ensure users can only access data relevant to their role:

  • Restaurant Owner: Full access to their restaurant data only
  • Sub-Admin: Limited access based on assigned permissions
  • Kitchen Staff: Access to orders and inventory only
  • Customers: Access only to their own order history
  • Admins: System-level access (internal team only)

5.2 Authentication Methods

  • Unique username/password combinations
  • Session tokens with expiration (24-hour max)
  • Optional OTP verification for sensitive operations
  • Activity logging for all data access

6. Multi-Tenant Data Isolation

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Feature: Our system enforces strict data isolation between restaurants. No restaurant can access another restaurant's data.

6.1 How Data Isolation Works

Every query in our system includes a filter by hotel_id. This ensures:

  • A restaurant owner can only see their own data
  • Customers see only their own orders
  • Staff see only orders for their assigned restaurant
  • Cross-restaurant data access is technically impossible

6.2 Data Isolation Verification

Our system implements multiple verification layers:

  • Query-level filtering by hotel_id
  • Session validation to ensure logged-in hotel matches requested data
  • Pre-verification loops to confirm data ownership before access
  • Audit logs tracking all data access attempts
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing

6.3 Guarantees

Guaranteed:
  • Restaurant A cannot access Restaurant B's orders
  • Restaurant A cannot access Restaurant B's customer data
  • Restaurant A cannot access Restaurant B's analytics
  • Cross-restaurant data leaks are prevented by system design

7. Data Retention & Deletion

7.1 Data Retention Policy

Data Type Retention Period Reason
Order History 3 years Tax, audit, and analytics
Payment Records 7 years Legal and financial compliance
Customer Data Active period + 2 years Relationship maintenance
Activity Logs 1 year Security and audit trails
Subscription Data Life of subscription + 1 year Billing and compliance

7.2 Account Deletion

When a restaurant owner requests account deletion or their account is deactivated for non-payment:

  • Account is marked as inactive immediately
  • QR codes stop functioning
  • Historical data (7 days) remains viewable
  • Full deletion occurs after 31 days of inactivity
  • Payment and legal records retained as per compliance requirements

8. Backup & Disaster Recovery

8.1 Backup Strategy

  • Daily automatic backups of all critical data
  • Monthly full system backups
  • Backups stored in secure, geographically distributed locations
  • Encrypted backup copies with separate access controls

8.2 Disaster Recovery

In case of data loss or system failure, we have recovery procedures in place to restore service within 24 hours. All backed-up data will be recovered with data isolation maintained.

10. User Rights & Control

10.1 Your Rights

All users have the following rights regarding their data:

  • Right to Access: Request copy of your data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request data deletion (within legal limits)
  • Right to Export: Download your data in standard format
  • Right to Object: Opt-out of certain data processing
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Stop data collection
